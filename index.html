Experience

I have BA (Hons) degrees in English & Comparative Literature from Goldsmiths College, and Acting from Guildhall. Between degrees I worked as a Transmission Controller, where I was responsible for the smooth management of live satellite TV channels, always making decisions under deadlines and time pressure. As an actor I've worked across all media, but mainly in audio dramas and video games. Companies include BBC Radio 4, Audible, Big Finish Productions, Sega, Sony, and Capcom.

In the last few years I started attending tech meetups and conferences in any and every area: from Javascript, to Serverless, to Ethics in AI, to Big Data; deciding to explore and understand the world of tech before pursuing a path in any specific specialisation. During this time I volunteered to work on Amnesty International's Decoders project - a microtasking app collating oil spill data in the Niger Delta. During this, I discovered that I was drawn to cloud and DevOps, and decided to get a grounding as a software engineer before moving into this area. As a member of various meetups I was recruited as the diversity and inclusion programme manager for the Monki Gras tech conference, where I was empowered to apply the best of what I'd experienced as a recipient of scholarship programmes. I was then accepted onto the BBC's Step Into Tech course with Tech Returners, a full stack programme where I got to work on solo and group projects. Since graduating I have kept up my coding skills, and am currently working my way through a cloud and DevOps roadmap. I became AWS Certified in November 2020.
